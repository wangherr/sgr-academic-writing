# Soul

Orientation, anticipation, cohesion.

# Goal

easy for the reader to understand what this chapter covers and what it aims to accomplish.

# Rule

No technical details. Assume a reader with general CS/AI background but no knowledge of your specific method. Avoid formulas, architecture diagrams, dataset names, or implementation specifics.

Keep it concise. The entire introduction should be one paragraph, at most two. It is a gateway, not a destination.

Natural transition. End with a sentence that bridges smoothly into the chapter body.

# Structure

The chapter introduction should follow a **three-part flow**:

### 1. Bridge from previous content
Briefly recall what the preceding chapter(s) established. One or two sentences is enough — enough to situate the reader, not to re-teach.

### 2. State the purpose
Explain what this chapter does and — more importantly — why it exists. What gap does it fill? What question does it answer? What goal does it serve in the larger thesis narrative?

This is the most important part: the reader needs to know the **destination** before they walk the path.

### 3. Preview the structure
Give a lightweight roadmap of what the chapter contains. Name the sections at a high level — topic labels, not technical breakdowns.
